I am a researcher in artificial intelligence at UCL, with a passion for human-centred machine learning, sustainability, and the societal and environmental impact of technology.

About my trajectory

During my PhD, I worked on novel ranking algorithms, and applied those in Spanish hospitals to create a model for donor-recipient matching in liver transplantation. Then I joined the Spanish National Research Council where I developed new pipelines for sustainable agriculture using drones and computer vision. After this I joined the University Loyola Andalucia as Assistant Professor. Among others, here I worked on a project with the European Space Agency on finding early warning signals for climate tipping points. After this I joined the Computer Lab at the University of Cambridge as a researcher, where I worked with neuroscientists to build Bayesian active learning models that could learn and adapt to our colour visual perception (for example for addressing visual deficiencies). The research was funded by Apple and it's currently used in many applications in computer graphics. I then joined UCL, where I am currently at. Among others, here I have focused on bridging the gap between generalisation analysis theory and practice, and on building humanly interpretable and interactive recomender systems for education at the intersection of HCI and AI. One of my most exciting collaborations lately was with the British Antarctic Survey, where a large team of climate and computer scientists came together to build a deep learning model for monitoring Arctic sea ice loss. Find more info in publications.

At the moment, all this is coming together into a research agenda on technology for sustainable development, spanning different applications on the environmental and social domain, with a socio-technical-ecological perspective.
