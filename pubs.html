Recent selected publications

Could AI Democratise Education? Socio-Technical Imaginaries of an EdTech Revolution, NeurIPS workshop on "Machine Learning for the Developing World", 2021.

Summary: In this work we ask what would it take (from a socio-technical standpoint) for a EdTech revolution to provide egalitarian and empowering access to education, beyond any political, cultural, language, geographical and learning ability barriers.

Seasonal Arctic Sea Ice Forecasting with Probabilistic Deep Learning, Nature Communications, 2021.
Summary: Anthropogenic warming has led to an unprecedented year-round reduction in Arctic sea ice. This has far-reaching consequences for indigenous and local communities, polar ecosystems, and global climate, motivating the need for accurate seasonal sea ice forecasts. In this work we present a probabilistic deep learning sea ice forecasting system.

Watch Less and Uncover More: Could Navigation Tools Help Users Search and Explore Videos?, ACM SIGIR Conference on Human Information Interaction and Retrieval, 2022.
Summary: This paper describes a novel user interface tool (the Content Flow Bar) at the intersection of AI and HCI, designed to allow users to quickly identify relevant fragments within informational videos to facilitate browsing, through a cognitively augmented form of navigation. We conducted a user study to evaluate how the tool changes the users search experience in video retrieval, as well as how it supports exploration and information seeking.

Progress in Self-Certified Neural Networks, NeurIPS workshop on "Bayesian Deep Learning", 2021.
Summary: An empirical analysis of the progress towards self-certified neural networks (where predictors are trained on all the available data and certified via a generalisation bound). With this approach we do not need held-out sets for certifying the performance of the predictor or hyperparameter tuning. We show that, at least in the small data regime, self-certified learning can be claimed to have been achieved! This is an important step towards statistically sound risk certificates of performance which do not suffer from sampling bias, having potential for governing machine learning algorithms' performance.

Tighter Risk Certificates for Neural Networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 2021.
Summary: This paper presents an empirical study regarding training probabilistic neural networks using training objectives derived from PAC-Bayes generalisation bounds. Our experiments show that our training methods produce very tight risk, showing promise not only to guide the learning algorithm through bounding the risk but also for model selection.

TrueLearn: A Family of Bayesian Algorithms to Match Lifelong Learners to Open Educational Resources , AAAI, 2020.
Summary: In a nutshell, the first transparent and human-centred educational recommender for educational materials!

From pairwise comparisons and rating to a unified quality scale, IEEE Transactions on Image Processing, 2019.
Summary: The goal of psychometric scaling is the quantification of perceptual experiences, understanding the relationship between an external stimulus, the internal representation and the response. We propose a probabilistic framework to fuse the outcome of different psychophysical experimental protocols, namely rating and pairwise comparisons. Such a method can be used for merging datasets of subjective nature and for experiments in which both measurements are collected. This is a stepping stone for computational social choice.
